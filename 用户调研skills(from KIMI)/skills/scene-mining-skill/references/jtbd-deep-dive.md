# JTBD Deep Dive — Complete Interview & Analysis Guide

## Switch Interview Guide (45-60 minutes)

### Section 1: Purchase Timeline (10 min)
Purpose: Reconstruct the decision-making context.

- "Take me back to the first time you thought about finding a solution for [problem]. What was happening that day?"
- "Where were you when you started looking? Were you alone?"
- "What was the first thing you did? Google search? Ask a colleague?"
- "How long did you look before making a decision? Hours, days, weeks?"
- "Did you have a deadline or external pressure driving the search?"

Probing technique: If vague, ask for specifics. "You said 'a few months ago' — was it before or after [specific event/holiday/quarter end]?"

### Section 2: Consideration Set (10 min)
Purpose: Understand competitive landscape from user's perspective.

- "What options did you consider? List them all — including 'do nothing' or manual approaches."
- "For each option you rejected: Why? What was the dealbreaker?"
- "Did you try any free trials or demos? What happened?"
- "Was there an option you wish existed but couldn't find?"
- "Who else was involved in the decision? What mattered to them?"

Probing technique: Watch for "nothing else" — usually means they didn't search deeply. Ask "Did you compare [specific competitor]?"

### Section 3: Decision Moment (10 min)
Purpose: Identify the trigger that caused commitment.

- "What was the specific moment you decided to go with [our product]?"
- "Was there a feature, a price point, a recommendation, or something else that tipped you?"
- "What were you most worried about when you signed up?"
- "Did you start with a free plan or trial, or did you pay immediately? Why?"

### Section 4: First Use (10 min)
Purpose: Validate whether expectations matched reality.

- "Walk me through your first use. What did you do first?"
- "Did you use a tutorial, documentation, or just explore?"
- "How long until you felt like you 'got it'?"
- "What almost made you give up?"
- "At what point did you think 'this is going to work for me'?"

### Section 5: Forces Analysis (10 min)
Purpose: Map the four forces that drive or block switching.

**Push (away from old solution)**:
- "What was the biggest frustration with [previous solution/process]?"
- "What was the final straw that made you start looking?"

**Pull (toward new solution)**:
- "What was most appealing about [our product] when you first saw it?"
- "What did you imagine your life would be like after switching?"

**Anxiety (about switching)**:
- "What worried you about making this change?"
- "What could have gone wrong?"
- "Did you worry about [data loss / learning curve / team adoption / cost]?"

**Habit (keeping from switching)**:
- "What almost kept you from switching?"
- "What was comfortable about your old way of doing things?"

### Section 6: Current State (10 min)
Purpose: Understand ongoing usage and satisfaction drivers.

- "How has your usage evolved since you started?"
- "What do you use most? What do you never use?"
- "If you had to convince a friend to use [product], what would you say?"
- "What would make you leave for a competitor?"

## Force Diagram Template

After each interview, map the forces:

```
OLD SOLUTION                              NEW SOLUTION
    ↑                                         ↑
    | Push: [What drove them away?]           | Pull: [What attracted them?]
    | Strength: 1-10                          | Strength: 1-10
    |                                         |
    | Habit: [What kept them?]                | Anxiety: [What worried them?]
    | Strength: 1-10                          | Strength: 1-10
    |                                         |
    ↓                                         ↓
  Inertia                                  Switch
```

Switch happens when: (Push + Pull) > (Anxiety + Habit)

## Job Statement Format

Synthesize each interview into a Job Statement:

```
When [situation/context],
I want to [functional job],
So I can [desired outcome],
And feel [emotional job],
And be perceived as [social job].
```

Example from Milkshake case:
"When I'm commuting alone in my car in the morning (situation), I want something to entertain me during the long drive (functional), so I can arrive at work in a good mood (emotional), and not feel like I wasted my time (social)."

## Analysis: Cross-Interview Synthesis

After conducting 8-15 interviews:

1. **Extract job statements**: Write one job statement per interview
2. **Cluster by similarity**: Group interviews with similar situations and desired outcomes
3. **Count frequency**: Which jobs appear across multiple interviews?
4. **Score by force strength**: Which jobs had the strongest Push+Pull signals?
5. **Identify overserved/underserved**: Where do current solutions exceed needs (over) or fail (under)?

Output: Ranked list of Job Clusters with frequency, force strength, and opportunity assessment.
